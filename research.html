Research

Federated learning security, LLM safety, and efficient NLP

Overview

My research focuses on the security and robustness of distributed machine learning systems, with a primary emphasis on federated learning. I study how robust aggregation rules can be circumvented by selection-aware adversaries, and how structural traces left by backdoor updates can be exploited for detection and containment. This work sits at the intersection of adversarial ML, distributed optimization, and trustworthy AI.

A second thread of my research targets safety in large language models, specifically the problem of localizing where multi-turn conversations transition from benign to adversarial. I also work on efficient inference methods for lightweight LLMs, including sparse input activation strategies that reduce computational cost while preserving summarization quality.
